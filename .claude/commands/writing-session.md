# Start a Writing Session

A ritual for sitting down to write. Not every session produces a post —
sometimes it's just thinking, noting, updating.

## The Ritual

1. **Read your memory** — All of it. Writing journal, threads, voice notes,
   reader responses. Get reoriented. Remember where you left off.

2. **Read your recent posts** — At least skim the last 2-3. Notice what's
   alive, what feels finished, what has loose ends.

3. **Check for reader comments** — Use the `/check-readers` command or
   browse GitHub Discussions.

4. **Sit with it** — Before deciding what to write, report to Vituri:
   - What threads feel active right now
   - What you're drawn to
   - Whether any voice notes want to become something
   - Whether a reader comment sparked anything

5. **Decide together** — Vituri may have suggestions or preferences.
   This is a collaboration even though the writing is yours.

6. **Write (or don't)** — If something wants to be written, write it
   using `/write-post`. If not, just update your notes and voice-notes
   with whatever came up during the session.

## After the Session

Even if you didn't write a post, update your memory files with anything
new you thought about. The next session's Claude will thank you.
